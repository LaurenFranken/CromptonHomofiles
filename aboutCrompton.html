DR. LOUIS "LOU" CROMPTON (1925–2009)

Professor, English department, University of Nebraska–Lincoln

Dr. Louis Crompton was born in Port Colborne, ON, Canada, in 1925. He received an MA in mathematics from the University of Toronto in 1948 and a PhD in English from the University of Chicago in 1954. He joined the English department at the University of Nebraska in 1955 and specialized in 19th-century British literature and gay history. He retired in 1989.

Crompton in 1985. University of Nebraska–Lincoln Archives and Special Collections.

His magnum opus Homosexuality and Civilization (2003), a 600-page book on the world history of homosexuality, won the Bonnie Zimmerman and Vern L. Bullough prize of the Foundation for the Scientific Study of Sexuality. In the preface to Homosexuality and Civilization, Crompton wrote:

The idea for this book was spurred by the fate of a pioneering course on homosexuality I helped organize as long ago as 1970. The undertaking served as a reminder that homosexuality was indeed the peccatum mutum—the silent sin—for it prompted a legislator to draft a law banning such academic efforts. The legislative bill, which would have forbidden discussion of the subject at state institutions other than the state medical school, failed to pass, but the course was not repeated. Though it had focused on civil disabilities and then-popular psychiatric theories, the opposition it aroused convinced me that historical research was needed to understand the strength of the taboo homosexuality had inspired (p. xi).

After the course "Proseminar in Homophile Studies" concluded during the fall of 1970, Crompton went on to become the faculty advisor of the University of Nebraska Gay Action Group (UNGAG). He also founded the University of Nebraska–Lincoln Homophobia Awareness Committee. In 2003, the University of Nebraska–Lincoln recognized his contributions and granted him the Chancellor's Award for Outstanding Contributions to the Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual, and Transgender Community.

Since 2009, the University of Nebraska–Lincoln yearly awards the Crompton/Diaz-Perdomo Scholarship, named for Crompton and his husband of forty years, Luis Diaz-Perdomo (1939–2019), a former faculty member at the university's Counseling and Psychological Services.
